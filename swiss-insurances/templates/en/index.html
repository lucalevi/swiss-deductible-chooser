How Swiss Health Insurance Works

In Switzerland, basic health insurance is mandatory for everyone. You can freely choose the insurance company (like choosing a phone provider), but the coverage itself is defined by law and is the same everywhere.

When you sign up, you also pick a franchise (deductible). This is the amount you pay yourself before insurance starts covering most of the costs. Once you reach this amount, your insurer pays the bills, but you still contribute a small part (10% co-insurance, capped at CHF 700 per year).

On top of this, you can choose different models to save money or get more services. For example:

  • Hausarzt model: you always visit your family doctor first.
  • Telemedizin model: you call a medical hotline before going to a doctor.

These models often reduce your monthly premium.

There are also hospital stay categories:

  • Allgemein: shared room, covered by basic insurance.
  • Halbprivat: two-bed room, often with choice of doctor.
  • Privat: private room and full freedom in choosing doctors.

These options, along with dental insurance, alternative medicine, or private wards, belong to supplementary insurance. They‚Äôre optional add-ons for more comfort or flexibility.
